What is Kamada Ekadashi?


Kamada Ekadashi is one of the 24 Ekadashis observed in a lunar year according to the Hindu calendar. This particular Ekadashi holds great significance as it is believed to bring prosperity, wealth, and fulfillment of desires. The word "Kamada" means desire, and this festival is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, the preserver of the universe, who is said to grant boons to devotees during this auspicious period.


The Legendary Story (Vrat Katha) of Kamada Ekadashi


The story of Kamada Ekadashi can be found in the Skanda Purana, one of the eighteen major Hindu texts. Once upon a time, there was a king named Ajatashatru who was known for his wickedness and lack of devotion towards Lord Vishnu. Despite numerous attempts by sages and deities to persuade him to change his ways, he remained unyielding.


One day, during the Chaitra (April-May) month, a sage named Bhrigu visited the king's court. Ajatashatru, disregarding the sage's divinity, ignored his pleas and continued with his evil deeds. Enraged by the king's behavior, Bhrigu cursed him to suffer from leprosy.


Upon realizing his mistake and seeking forgiveness, Ajatashatru embarked on a journey to find Lord Vishnu. After much hardship, he eventually met the deity at a place called Kamalalaya (the abode of the lotus). Lord Vishnu, moved by the king's devotion and remorse, granted him salvation and cured his leprosy.


Since then, it is believed that those who observe Kamada Ekadashi with devotion and faith will also receive Lord Vishnu's blessings and be freed from their sins.


Rituals, Mantras, and Parana Timings for Kamada Ekadashi in 2026


On the day of Kamada Ekadashi, devotees observe a fast throughout the day and perform specific rituals to seek Lord Vishnu's blessings. Some of these include:


  • Waking up early and taking a bath before sunrise
  • Chanting the mantra "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya" 108 times
  • Reading the Bhagavad Gita or other Hindu scriptures
  • Offering prayers to Lord Vishnu using lotus flowers, incense, and fruits
  • Breaking the fast after moonrise by offering food to the poor and needy

It is essential to check the parana time (time to break the fast) as it varies based on location. Generally, it occurs between sunset and the end of the tithi (lunar day). For 2026, the parana time will be announced closer to the event.


Significance and Benefits of Observing Kamada Ekadashi Vrat


Observing Kamada Ekadashi is said to bring prosperity, wealth, good health, and fulfillment of desires. The festival is also believed to help alleviate leprosy, skin diseases, and other ailments. By devoting oneself to Lord Vishnu during this period, one can gain his blessings and attain spiritual growth.
