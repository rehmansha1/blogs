Palm Sunday Traditions and Significance


Palm Sunday is a time for Christians worldwide to reflect upon Jesus Christ's entry into Jerusalem, commemorating the start of His final days leading up to crucifixion and resurrection. The tradition of waving palm branches originates from the crowds that greeted Jesus with palm branches and cloaks as He rode on a donkey, symbolizing His arrival as a king. Today, many churches distribute palm leaves or crosses for parishioners to carry during the procession, honoring this ancient practice.
